Hi there!

My name is Urvi Ashturkar. I have always cared about user-centred design, though I didn't find words for my intuition until four years ago when I read Don Norman's The Design of Everyday Things. Even as a kid, I was excited about building fun solutions that worked for those minor problems that inevitably crop up in the house. My friends say I am incredibly articulate in explaining my internal experiences, thoughts, and behaviours. The same insight serves me well when attempting to understand others' worlds - whether designing for them or working with them.

Four years ago, as an undergraduate freshman, I started teaching myself graphic design, video production, UX research, and design methodologies. I knew my work was good when the people I worked with started referring me to their acquaintances to commission projects. In addition, my degree in computer engineering has equipped me with analytical thinking skills and unique insight into the developer's perspective and vocabulary.

Currently, I am working on web application design for client projects for an IT firm in Pune, India.



Here's what I'm like at work -

  • When I'm talking to you, I will speak your language.
  • My work is organized and well-documented.
  • I notice, think about and work on all the most minor details.
  • I keep stakeholders in the loop about what I am doing and why.
  • I respect your time and mine.
  • There is a lot I don't know. I will ask questions and quickly learn anything I need to.
